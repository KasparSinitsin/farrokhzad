Who was Fereydoon?

Fereydoon Farrokhzad (1938-1992) was a legendary Iranian artist known for his multifaceted talents as a singer, poet, political activist, actor, and television host.

He gained widespread recognition for his courageous stance against human rights abuses and his efforts to rescue POW children during the Iran-Iraq war.

Fereydoon's literary contributions included the publication of several acclaimed poetry collections in Persian and German languages.

As the host of the popular television program "Silver Carnation" in the 1970s, he reached millions of viewers with his engaging interviews, musical performances, and cultural commentary, solidifying his status as a beloved figure in Iranian media.

Throughout his life, Fereydoon remained dedicated to using his platform to advocate for social justice and freedom of expression, leaving behind a lasting legacy as a beacon of courage and integrity in the face of adversity.
